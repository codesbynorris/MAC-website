About Grace Kinga

Grace Kinga is an inspiring author, advocate, and mother whose journey has touched the lives of many. Her book, "My Daughter, You Can Fly, Even With a Broken Wing," shares the powerful story of her daughter's resilience and triumph over physical limitations, becoming a source of hope and encouragement for families facing similar challenges.

Grace is passionate about empowering individuals with disabilities and promoting inclusivity in all aspects of life. Through her writing, speaking engagements, and advocacy work, she strives to raise awareness, challenge perceptions, and create a world where everyone can reach their full potential. She believes in the power of community and the importance of accessible resources for individuals and families navigating the complexities of disability.

Beyond her work as an author and advocate, Grace is a dedicated mother and a pillar of strength for her family. Her unwavering belief in her daughter's abilities, coupled with her tireless efforts to provide support and access to opportunities, has been instrumental in her daughter's remarkable achievements. Grace's story is a testament to the power of love, resilience, and the unwavering belief that every individual, regardless of their challenges, has the potential to soar.

Grace is actively involved in disability rights organizations and support groups. She is committed to increasing accessibility, promoting inclusive education, supporting families with disabilities.
